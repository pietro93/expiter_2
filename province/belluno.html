Overview

The province of Belluno is the 92nd largest Italian province by population with 202.950 people, located in the Veneto region.

The larger Belluno metropolitan area comprises 61 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 36.102 km2. The population density is 56 inhabitants per km2, making it sparcely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.07.

Belluno is a city in Veneto, Italy.

Climate

The province of Belluno receives on average 198 hours of sunshine per month, or 6.6 hours of sunshine per day. This is 16.10% less than the average for Italy and 12.50% less than the average for the region of Veneto.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 8.88 days per month, which is well above average for an Italian province.

Throughout the autumn and winter season, there are usually 3.35 days per month with fog and 7.73 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C. In the summer, there are on average 16.48 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Belluno is around 1850.21€, which is higher than the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 2272.42€ per month for an individual or 3368.29€ per month for a family of 4. The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 933.57€ per month.

Overall, Belluno is expensive compared to other Italian provinces. Living in Belluno is around 37.71% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Belluno is above average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 8.72 general practitioners and 23.4 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Belluno is 82 years of age.

Education

Belluno has a higher-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 68.7%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 24.4%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.47, which is not far from the national average of 10.44. There are no universities in this province.

Leisure



Overall, Belluno has somewhat decent nightlife with 3.88 bars and 3.03 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Belluno is overall moderately safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 2348.8 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 17.17% lower than the national average.

There have been around 1.3 deadly road accidents and 9.6 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Belluno. This is respectively 140.74% more driving accidents than average and 25.58% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 90.87% lower than average with only 6.44 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 19.37% lower than average with 141.11 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 141.11 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 84.33% less the national average.

Overall, Belluno is quite safe to live and travel to.

Transport

Public transport in Belluno is lacking, and traffic is very high. There are on average 0.72 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 1.60km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Belluno consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Belluno somewhat bike-friendly by Italian standards.
