Overview

The province of Teramo is the 72nd largest Italian province by population with 308.052 people, located in the Abruzzo region.

The larger Teramo metropolitan area comprises 47 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 195.438 km2. The population density is 158 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.05.

Teramo is the capital of the province of Teramo in the Italian Region of Abruzzo.

Climate

The province of Teramo receives on average 237 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.9 hours of sunshine per day. This is 0.42% more than the average for Italy and 0.94% less than the average for the region of Abruzzo.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.84 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

Throughout the autumn and winter season, there are usually 0.32 days per month with fog and 1.31 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C. In the summer, there are on average 24 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Teramo is around 1374.63€, which is lower than the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1539.2€ per month for an individual or 2294.96€ per month for a family of 4. The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 659.72€ per month.

Overall, Teramo is affordable compared to other Italian provinces. Living in Teramo is around 6.72% cheaper than the average of all Italian provinces.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Teramo is below average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 10.11 general practitioners and 23.1 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Teramo is very high at 82.3 years of age.

Education

Teramo has a lower-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 61.3%; and a higher-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 31.7%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.51, which is not far from the national average of 10.44. There is one university in the province.

Leisure



Overall, Teramo has somewhat decent nightlife with 3.22 bars and 4.98 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Teramo is overall very safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 3086.1 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 8.83% higher than the national average.

There have been around 0.2 deadly road accidents and 15.3 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Teramo. This is respectively 62.96% less driving accidents than average and -18.60% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 4.99% lower than average with only 67.01 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 12.07% higher than average with 196.15 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 196.15 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 30.94% less the national average.

Overall, Teramo is a very safe place to visit and live in.

Transport

Public transport in Teramo is lacking, and traffic is very high. There are on average 0.72 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 0.19km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Teramo consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Teramo not very bike-friendly.
