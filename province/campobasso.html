Overview

The province of Campobasso is the 86th largest Italian province by population with 221.238 people, located in the Molise region.

The larger Campobasso metropolitan area comprises 84 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 292.541 km2. The population density is 76 inhabitants per km2, making it sparcely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.05.

Campobasso is the capital of Molise region.

The province of Campobasso receives on average 222 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.4 hours of sunshine per day. This is 5.93% less than the average for Italy and 0.68% more than the average for the region of Molise.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 7.19 days per month, which is an ordinary amount of precipitation for an Italian province.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Campobasso is below average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 10.44 general practitioners and 26.6 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Campobasso is very high at 82.1 years of age.

Education

Campobasso has a lower-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 60.8%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 25.9%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.35, which is not far from the national average of 10.44. There is one university in the province.

Leisure



Overall, Campobasso has somewhat decent nightlife with 3.14 bars and 4.01 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Campobasso is overall moderately safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 2168 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 23.55% lower than the national average.

There have been around 0.9 deadly road accidents and 13.1 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Campobasso. This is respectively 66.67% more driving accidents than average and -1.55% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 88.67% higher than average with 133.07 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 46.70% lower than average with 93.29 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 93.29 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 66.94% less the national average.

Campobasso is considered a safe city with a relatively low crime rate. Instances of petty theft and pickpocketing can occur, especially in crowded tourist areas, so it is advisable to remain vigilant and keep personal belongings secure. Campobasso maintains effective safety measures and has a visible police presence. Road accidents are infrequent, but it is important to exercise caution and adhere to traffic regulations.
