The province of Isernia receives on average 219 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.3 hours of sunshine per day. This is 7.20% less than the average for Italy and 0.68% less than the average for the region of Molise.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 8.11 days per month, which is well above average for an Italian province.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Isernia is below average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 12.33 general practitioners and 31.5 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Isernia is 81.7 years of age.

Education

Isernia has a higher-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 66.5%; and a higher-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 35.9%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.47, which is not far from the national average of 10.44. There are no universities in this province.

Leisure



Overall, Isernia has somewhat decent nightlife with 3.55 bars and 3.97 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Isernia is overall moderately safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 2609.3 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 7.99% lower than the national average.

There have been around 1.2 deadly road accidents and 15 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Isernia. This is respectively 122.22% more driving accidents than average and -16.28% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 74.55% lower than average with only 17.95 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 55.57% lower than average with 77.76 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 77.76 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 67.57% less the national average.

Isernia generally maintains a low crime rate, providing a safe environment for residents and visitors. However, it is advisable to exercise caution and follow basic safety practices. Being aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas and tourist spots, can contribute to a safer experience in Isernia.

Transport

Public transport in Isernia is lacking, and traffic is very high. There are on average 0.79 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 0.35km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Isernia consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Isernia not very bike-friendly.
