Overview

The larger Sassari metropolitan area comprises 92 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 769.175 km2. The population density is 64 inhabitants per km2, making it sparcely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.05.

The province of Sassari receives on average 246 hours of sunshine per month, or 8.2 hours of sunshine per day. This is 4.24% more than the average for Italy and 0.49% more than the average for the region of Sardinia.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.76 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Sassari is above average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 10.12 general practitioners and 34.5 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Sassari is 81.8 years of age.

Education

Sassari has a lower-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 49.4%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 15.9%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 9.86, which is lower than the national average of 10.44. There is one university in the province.

Leisure

Sassari, a lively city in northern Sardinia, offers a vibrant and energetic nightlife that reflects its youthful population and dynamic atmosphere. The city's historic center is filled with charming bars, pubs, and clubs, creating a lively social scene after dark. Sassari hosts various cultural events, music festivals, and live performances throughout the year, attracting both locals and visitors.

Overall, Sassari has pretty good nightlife with 4.53 bars and 5.6 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Sassari is overall very safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 2748 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 3.09% lower than the national average.

There have been around 0.9 deadly road accidents and 12.5 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Sassari. This is respectively 66.67% more driving accidents than average and 3.10% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 44.99% lower than average with only 38.8 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 37.69% lower than average with 109.06 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 109.06 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 53.88% less the national average.

Sassari is generally considered a safe city to visit. While it maintains a relatively low crime rate, like any urban area, instances of petty theft and pickpocketing can occur, especially in crowded places and public transportation. Travelers are advised to take basic precautions such as keeping personal belongings secure and remaining vigilant in tourist areas. Sassari has a moderate level of street crime, but violent crimes are relatively rare. Road accidents are infrequent, but it's important to adhere to traffic regulations and exercise caution while driving.

Transport

Public transport in Sassari is quite good, and traffic is somewhat high. There are on average 0.67 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 0.05km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Sassari consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Sassari not very bike-friendly.
