Overview

The province of Pisa is the 46th largest Italian province by population with 419.037 people, located in the Tuscany region. The city of Pisa is located in the Tuscany region of Italy. It is famous for its iconic leaning tower, part of the Piazza dei Miracoli (Square of Miracles), which also includes the Pisa Cathedral and the Baptistry. Pisa is a historic city with a rich cultural heritage, known for its prestigious university, the University of Pisa, which was established in the 12th century.

The larger Pisa metropolitan area comprises 37 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 244.472 km2. The population density is 171 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.06.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Pisa is above average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 10.95 general practitioners and 47.8 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Pisa is very high at 82.9 years of age.

Education

Pisa has a higher-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 68.2%; and a higher-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 29%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 11.04, which is above the national average of 10.44. There is one university in the province.

Leisure

Pisa offers a vibrant nightlife scene that caters to both locals and tourists. The city is dotted with lively bars, pubs, and clubs where visitors can enjoy a variety of drinks and music genres. Whether you're looking for a casual evening with friends or a night of dancing, Pisa has options to suit different tastes and preferences. The historic surroundings add a charming backdrop to the city's nightlife, creating a memorable experience.

Overall, Pisa has somewhat decent nightlife with 2.54 bars and 4.47 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Pisa is overall moderately safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 3219.9 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 13.55% higher than the national average.

There have been around 0.6 deadly road accidents and 16.2 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Pisa. This is respectively 11.11% more driving accidents than average and -25.58% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 58.70% lower than average with only 29.13 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 63.98% higher than average with 286.99 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are not totally uncommon, around 35.82% higher than average with 30.07 reports per 100k inhabitants.

Overall, Pisa is quite safe to live and travel to.

Transport

Public transport in Pisa is quite good, and traffic is below average. There are on average 0.63 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.
