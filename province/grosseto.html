Overview

The province of Grosseto is the 85th largest Italian province by population with 221.629 people, located in the Tuscany region.

The larger Grosseto metropolitan area comprises 28 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 450.312 km2. The population density is 49 inhabitants per km2, making it sparcely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.08.

Grosseto is a small city in the Tuscany region of Italy.

Climate

The province of Grosseto receives on average 261 hours of sunshine per month, or 8.7 hours of sunshine per day. This is 10.59% more than the average for Italy and 9.16% more than the average for the region of Tuscany.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.13 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Grosseto is above average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 10.75 general practitioners and 27.5 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Grosseto is very high at 83.1 years of age.

Education

Grosseto has a higher-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 63%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 25.3%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.2, which is not far from the national average of 10.44. There are no universities in this province.

Leisure



Overall, Grosseto has somewhat decent nightlife with 3.47 bars and 6.74 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Grosseto is overall very safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 3075.8 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 8.46% higher than the national average.

There have been around 0.5 deadly road accidents and 20.4 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Grosseto. This is respectively 7.41% less driving accidents than average and -58.14% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 76.88% lower than average with only 16.31 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 27.84% higher than average with 223.75 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 223.75 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 44.76% less the national average.

Overall, Grosseto is a very safe place to visit and live in.

Transport

Public transport in Grosseto is lacking, and traffic is somewhat high. There are on average 0.66 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 1.64km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Grosseto consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Grosseto somewhat bike-friendly by Italian standards.
