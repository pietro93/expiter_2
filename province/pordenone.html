Overview

The province of Pordenone is the 71st largest Italian province by population with 312.533 people, located in the Friuli-Venezia Giulia region.

The larger Pordenone metropolitan area comprises 50 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 227.542 km2. The population density is 137 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.04.

Pordenone is a city in the northeastern Friuli-Venezia Giulia region of Italy. This article also covers the town of Aviano.

Climate

The province of Pordenone receives on average 225 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.5 hours of sunshine per day. This is 4.66% less than the average for Italy and 1.32% less than the average for the region of Friuli-Venezia Giulia.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 8.72 days per month, which is well above average for an Italian province.

Throughout the autumn and winter season, there are usually 3.3 days per month with fog and 1.84 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C. In the summer, there are on average 20.8 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Pordenone is around 1942.74€, which is higher than the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1810.24€ per month for an individual or 2728.91€ per month for a family of 4. The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 783.75€ per month.

Overall, Pordenone is expensive compared to other Italian provinces. Living in Pordenone is around 9.70% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Pordenone is below average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around undefined pharmacies, 7.73 general practitioners and 25.6 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants. Average life expectancy in Pordenone is very high at 83 years of age.

Education

Pordenone has a higher-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 70.9%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 24.4%. The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.66, which is not far from the national average of 10.44. There are no universities in this province.

Leisure



Overall, Pordenone has somewhat decent nightlife with 2.41 bars and 3.11 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.

Crime and Safety

The province of Pordenone is overall very safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 1841.2 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 35.07% lower than the national average.

There have been around 0.3 deadly road accidents and 8.6 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Pordenone. This is respectively 44.44% less driving accidents than average and 33.33% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 92.74% lower than average with only 5.12 cases per 100k inhabitants. Reports of house thefts are 18.48% lower than average with 142.67 cases per 100k inhabitants. Cases of robbery are uncommon with 142.67 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 73.98% less the national average.

Pordenone is known for its safety and low crime rate. Instances of petty theft and pickpocketing are uncommon, ensuring a secure experience for residents and visitors. Pordenone benefits from effective safety measures and a reliable police presence. Road accidents are relatively infrequent, but it is important to exercise caution and follow traffic regulations when driving.

Transport

Public transport in Pordenone is lacking, and traffic is very high. There are on average 0.73 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 1.72km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Pordenone consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Pordenone somewhat bike-friendly by Italian standards.
