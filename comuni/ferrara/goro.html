Goro is a municipality of 3.588 inhabitants located in the Ferrara province in the Italian region of Emilia-Romagna in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 135 people per km² and an altitude of 1 metres above the sea level.

Goro accounts for about 1.04% of the total population in the province of Ferrara and about 0.00595% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Goro is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Ferrara experiences on average 83.49 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 15.50 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 87.36 days per year. There are 53.01 foggy days throughout the year. Goro receives around 7.7 hours of sunshine per day on average.
