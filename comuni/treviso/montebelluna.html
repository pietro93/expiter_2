Montebelluna is a municipality of 31.058 inhabitants located in the Treviso province in the Italian region of Veneto in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 634 people per km² and an altitude of 109 metres above the sea level.

Montebelluna accounts for about 3.50% of the total population in the province of Treviso and about 0.05154% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Montebelluna is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Treviso experiences on average 73.41 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 7.71 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 102.72 days per year. There are 21.91 foggy days throughout the year. Montebelluna receives around 7.6 hours of sunshine per day on average.
