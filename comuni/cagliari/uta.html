Uta is a municipality of 8.585 inhabitants located in the Cagliari province in the Italian region of Sardinia in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 64 people per km² and an altitude of 6 metres above the sea level.

Uta accounts for about 1.99% of the total population in the province of Cagliari and about 0.01425% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Uta is classified as a Climate Zone C, which means it is a fairly warm location. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Cagliari experiences on average 84.62 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.00 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 67.20 days per year. There are 6.69 foggy days throughout the year. Uta receives around 8.5 hours of sunshine per day on average.
