Bassano Romano is a municipality of 4.592 inhabitants located in the Viterbo province in the Italian region of Lazio in Central Italy. It has a population density of 122 people per km² and an altitude of 360 metres above the sea level.

Bassano Romano accounts for about 1.45% of the total population in the province of Viterbo and about 0.00762% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Bassano Romano is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters with hot and somewhat windy summers and overall little precipitation.

The province of Viterbo experiences on average 72.51 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 4.49 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 87.60 days per year. There are 4.59 foggy days throughout the year. Bassano Romano receives around 8.3 hours of sunshine per day on average.
