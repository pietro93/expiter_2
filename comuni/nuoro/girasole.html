Girasole is a municipality of 1.326 inhabitants located in the Nuoro province in the Italian region of Sardinia in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 101 people per km² and an altitude of 8 metres above the sea level.

Girasole accounts for about 0.64% of the total population in the province of Nuoro and about 0.00220% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Girasole is classified as a Climate Zone B, which means it is one of the warmest and sunniest locations in Italy. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Nuoro experiences on average 68.09 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 5.79 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 86.88 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Girasole receives around 7.7 hours of sunshine per day on average.
