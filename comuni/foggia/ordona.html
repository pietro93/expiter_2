Ordona is a municipality of 2.909 inhabitants located in the Foggia province in the Italian region of Apulia in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 74 people per km² and an altitude of 120 metres above the sea level.

Ordona accounts for about 0.47% of the total population in the province of Foggia and about 0.00483% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Ordona is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by cold winters and hot but windy summers, with somewhat frequent rainfall spread throughout the year.

The province of Foggia experiences on average 82.90 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 2.50 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 83.76 days per year. There are 10.70 foggy days throughout the year. Ordona receives around 8.1 hours of sunshine per day on average.
