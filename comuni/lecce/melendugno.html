Melendugno is a municipality of 9.969 inhabitants located in the Lecce province in the Italian region of Apulia in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 108 people per km² and an altitude of 36 metres above the sea level.

Melendugno accounts for about 1.25% of the total population in the province of Lecce and about 0.01654% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Melendugno is classified as a Climate Zone C, which means it is a fairly warm location. The local climate is characterized by cold winters and hot but windy summers, with somewhat frequent rainfall spread throughout the year.

The province of Lecce experiences on average 89.21 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.51 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 81.36 days per year. There are 24.89 foggy days throughout the year. Melendugno receives around 8.3 hours of sunshine per day on average.
