Berchidda is a municipality of 2.636 inhabitants located in the Sassari province in the Italian region of Sardinia in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 13 people per km² and an altitude of 290 metres above the sea level.

Berchidda accounts for about 0.54% of the total population in the province of Sassari and about 0.00437% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Berchidda is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Sassari experiences on average 82.22 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.31 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 81.12 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Berchidda receives around 8.2 hours of sunshine per day on average.
