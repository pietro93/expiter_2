Biandrate is a municipality of 1.297 inhabitants located in the Novara province in the Italian region of Piedmont in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 104 people per km² and an altitude of 160 metres above the sea level.

Biandrate accounts for about 0.35% of the total population in the province of Novara and about 0.00215% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Biandrate is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Novara experiences on average 65.79 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 17.01 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 91.20 days per year. There are 40.90 foggy days throughout the year. Biandrate receives around 7.5 hours of sunshine per day on average.
