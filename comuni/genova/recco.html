Recco is a municipality of 9.442 inhabitants located in the Genova province in the Italian region of Liguria in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 966 people per km² and an altitude of 5 metres above the sea level.

Recco accounts for about 1.13% of the total population in the province of Genova and about 0.01567% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Recco is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters with hot and somewhat windy summers and overall little precipitation.

The province of Genova experiences on average 42.79 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 2.71 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 88.68 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Recco receives around 7.5 hours of sunshine per day on average.
