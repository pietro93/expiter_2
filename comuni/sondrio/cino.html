Cino is a municipality of 331 inhabitants located in the Sondrio province in the Italian region of Lombardy in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 65 people per km² and an altitude of 504 metres above the sea level.

Cino accounts for about 0.18% of the total population in the province of Sondrio and about 0.00055% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Cino is classified as a Climate Zone F, which means it is one of the coldest locations in Italy. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Sondrio experiences on average 63.39 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 14.50 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 103.20 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Cino receives around 7.1 hours of sunshine per day on average.
