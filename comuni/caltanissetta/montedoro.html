Montedoro is a municipality of 1.393 inhabitants located in the Caltanissetta province in the Italian region of Sicily in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 96 people per km² and an altitude of 450 metres above the sea level.

Montedoro accounts for about 0.53% of the total population in the province of Caltanissetta and about 0.00231% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Montedoro is classified as a Climate Zone C, which means it is a fairly warm location. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Caltanissetta experiences on average 75.50 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 2.09 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 78.84 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Montedoro receives around 8.4 hours of sunshine per day on average.
