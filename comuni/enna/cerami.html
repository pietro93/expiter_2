Cerami is a municipality of 1.839 inhabitants located in the Enna province in the Italian region of Sicily in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 19 people per km² and an altitude of 970 metres above the sea level.

Cerami accounts for about 1.12% of the total population in the province of Enna and about 0.00305% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Cerami is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Enna experiences on average 26.50 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 12.62 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 84.24 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Cerami receives around 8.3 hours of sunshine per day on average.
