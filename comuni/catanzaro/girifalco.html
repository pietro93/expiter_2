Girifalco is a municipality of 5.538 inhabitants located in the Catanzaro province in the Italian region of Calabria in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 129 people per km² and an altitude of 456 metres above the sea level.

Girifalco accounts for about 1.55% of the total population in the province of Catanzaro and about 0.00919% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Girifalco is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Catanzaro experiences on average 88.11 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.41 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 83.28 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Girifalco receives around 8.5 hours of sunshine per day on average.
