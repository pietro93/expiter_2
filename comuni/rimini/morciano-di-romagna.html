Morciano di Romagna is a municipality of 7.161 inhabitants located in the Rimini province in the Italian region of Emilia-Romagna in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 1.316 people per km² and an altitude of 83 metres above the sea level.

Morciano di Romagna accounts for about 2.12% of the total population in the province of Rimini and about 0.01188% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Morciano di Romagna is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Rimini experiences on average 49.20 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 7.10 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 85.08 days per year. There are 25.20 foggy days throughout the year. Morciano di Romagna receives around 7.9 hours of sunshine per day on average.
