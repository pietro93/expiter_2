Aulla is a municipality of 10.781 inhabitants located in the Massa-Carrara province in the Italian region of Tuscany in Central Italy. It has a population density of 180 people per km² and an altitude of 64 metres above the sea level.

Aulla accounts for about 5.53% of the total population in the province of Massa-Carrara and about 0.01789% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Aulla is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters with hot and somewhat windy summers and overall little precipitation.

The province of Massa-Carrara experiences on average 80.50 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 1.41 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 93.00 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Aulla receives around 8 hours of sunshine per day on average.
