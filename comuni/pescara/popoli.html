Popoli is a municipality of 4.784 inhabitants located in the Pescara province in the Italian region of Abruzzo in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 137 people per km² and an altitude of 254 metres above the sea level.

Popoli accounts for about 1.50% of the total population in the province of Pescara and about 0.00794% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Popoli is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by cold winters and hot but windy summers, with somewhat frequent rainfall spread throughout the year.

The province of Pescara experiences on average 48.21 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 2.50 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 75.84 days per year. There are 11.11 foggy days throughout the year. Popoli receives around 8.3 hours of sunshine per day on average.
