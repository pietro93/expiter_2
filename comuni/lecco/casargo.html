Casargo is a municipality of 841 inhabitants located in the Lecco province in the Italian region of Lombardy in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 43 people per km² and an altitude of 804 metres above the sea level.

Casargo accounts for about 0.25% of the total population in the province of Lecco and about 0.00140% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Casargo is classified as a Climate Zone F, which means it is one of the coldest locations in Italy. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Lecco experiences on average 57.50 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 10.70 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 113.28 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Casargo receives around 7 hours of sunshine per day on average.
