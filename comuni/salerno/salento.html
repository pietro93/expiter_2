Salento is a municipality of 1.842 inhabitants located in the Salerno province in the Italian region of Campania in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 77 people per km² and an altitude of 420 metres above the sea level.

Salento accounts for about 0.17% of the total population in the province of Salerno and about 0.00306% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Salento is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters with hot and somewhat windy summers and overall little precipitation.

The province of Salerno experiences on average 73.30 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.21 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 95.04 days per year. There is little to no fog throughout the year. Salento receives around 8.2 hours of sunshine per day on average.
