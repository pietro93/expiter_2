Barbania is a municipality of 1.571 inhabitants located in the Turin province in the Italian region of Piedmont in Northern Italy. It has a population density of 123 people per km² and an altitude of 362 metres above the sea level.

Barbania accounts for about 0.07% of the total population in the province of Turin and about 0.00261% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Barbania is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Turin experiences on average 59.69 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 13.61 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 87.12 days per year. There are 7.99 foggy days throughout the year. Barbania receives around 7.5 hours of sunshine per day on average.
